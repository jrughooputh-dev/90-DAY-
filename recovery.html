01

Daily Morning Protocol

How you start the first 90 minutes of your day determines the biochemical environment your body operates in for the next 16 hours.
Wake-to-noon sequence — adapted to your schedule (wake 10–11am)
On wakingWater first. 500ml still water immediately. Add a pinch of sea salt or an electrolyte tab — you are dehydrated from 6+ hours of sleep and your kidneys need sodium to retain the water. Do not reach for a cigarette. Set a 15-minute rule: no smoking until at least 15 minutes after waking.
+10 minMorning light. Step outside or stand by a bright window for 5 minutes. Natural morning light resets your circadian clock, which is currently misaligned. This is the single most effective action for gradually shifting your sleep timing forward. It triggers cortisol to peak at the right time and primes melatonin to rise earlier in the evening.
+20 minCoffee or tea. Black coffee is fine and beneficial — it increases metabolic rate, improves focus, and contains antioxidants. Do not add sugar. Avoid sweetened drinks entirely. Coffee before food on an empty stomach is acceptable for most people.
+60–90 minMeal 1 — protein anchor. 3 whole eggs + 2 whites + 60g oats + Greek yogurt. Add turmeric and black pepper to the oats. This is non-negotiable. Skipping this meal means 4–5 hours of cortisol-driven catabolism — your body burns muscle for energy and fat accumulation accelerates.
Post-mealSupplements. Take Vitamin C (500mg), Zinc (15mg), B-Complex, Vitamin D3 with your first meal. These replace what smoking depletes daily and need food for absorption.
Any time AM20-min walk. Do this without smoking. This is now your clean walk. It primes your cardiovascular system, burns fat while glycogen is partially depleted from sleep, and establishes a movement habit anchor for the rest of the day.
02

Smoking Detox Protocol

You cannot out-supplement a 3–5 session daily habit. The protocol below reduces the damage and accelerates the recovery — but reduction is the engine.

Each cigarette or combustion session generates approximately 4,000+ chemical compounds including acrolein, formaldehyde, benzene, and carbon monoxide. These deplete antioxidants, damage lung epithelial tissue, elevate cortisol, suppress MPS, and trigger psoriatic pathways. The detox protocol is designed to aggressively replenish what combustion strips away and support cellular repair on a daily basis.

Smoking reduction roadmap — 90 days
Days 1–30
Delay and decouple
Push first session to 15 min after waking minimum. Remove the walk-smoke habit completely — walk is clean. Reduce from 3–5 sessions to 2–3. Switch to rolling tobacco only (no additives) if not already.
Days 31–60
Eliminate combustion for THC
Switch THC delivery to a dry herb vaporiser at 170–185°C. This delivers cannabinoids while eliminating the combustion products responsible for 90% of the lung and inflammatory damage. Tobacco: reduce to 1–2 sessions per day.
Days 61–90
Tobacco to near-zero
By Day 60 your training performance will have visibly improved, your skin will be noticeably better, and your sleep will be deeper. These are self-reinforcing. Tobacco: 0–1 per day. The physical dependence has substantially reduced by this point — what remains is habit and association.
Day 90+
Post-quit lung timeline
12hrs: CO clears, O2 capacity restores. 72hrs: cilia regrow in bronchial lining. 2–4 weeks: lung function measurably improves. 3 months: MPS normalises, training results accelerate. 1 year: cardiovascular risk halved.
Craving management — practical tools
Deep breathing (4-7-8 method)
Inhale 4 counts, hold 7, exhale 8. Activates parasympathetic nervous system. Use when a craving peaks — cravings peak at 3–5 min then pass.
Cold water on wrists during craving
Reduces cortisol spike associated with withdrawal. Simple and immediate.
Delay by 5 minutes
Tell yourself you will smoke in 5 minutes. Most cravings pass before the 5 minutes are up.
Chew gum or drink water instead
The oral fixation component of smoking is real. Chewing gum or drinking water addresses the physical urge without the damage.
Track sessions in your journal
Just logging how many you had today creates awareness and accountability. You cannot manage what you do not measure.

Muscle Recovery Protocol

Post-training recovery checklist — do this every session
Eat within 45 minutes of finishing
30–40g protein + 30–50g carbs. MPS is maximally elevated in this window. Greek yogurt + banana or rice + chicken. This is your most impactful nutritional decision of the day.
Shower immediately
Within 20 minutes. Lukewarm water — hot water on psoriasis patches causes irritation. Fragrance-free wash. Apply moisturiser within 3 minutes of stepping out.
No ibuprofen within 4 hours post-training
NSAIDs blunt the inflammatory signal required for muscle repair. If you are in pain, ice and elevation first. Paracetamol is acceptable as it does not interfere with MPS.
Drink 500ml water post-session
You lose 500ml–1L of water through sweat in a 30-minute bodyweight session. Hydration is directly required for protein synthesis and glycogen replenishment.
Do not train same muscle group if still sore
Significant soreness = active repair in progress. Training on top of this does not produce double the stimulus — it disrupts the repair process. 48–72 hours is the minimum recovery window.
Casein protein or cottage cheese before 10:30pm
180g cottage cheese = 20g slow-digesting casein. Feeds muscle repair through sleep for 6–8 hours. Growth hormone pulses during deep sleep — you need amino acids available during this window.
Magnesium glycinate 300mg before sleep
Deepens slow-wave sleep — the phase where 70% of growth hormone is released. Also reduces muscle cramping and DOMS. Take 45 minutes before you want to sleep.
Active recovery — what to do on rest days
20–30 min walkLow-intensity movement increases blood flow to recovering muscles, accelerating nutrient delivery and waste removal without adding training stress. This is not optional recovery — it is active recovery.
Stretching 10 minFocus: hip flexors (damaged by sitting), hamstrings, chest/pec. Video: search "10 min full body stretch routine" on YouTube. Improves range of motion needed for Phase 2 and 3 exercises.
Cold water finishEnd your shower with 30–60 seconds of cold water. Reduces systemic inflammation, improves circulation, and has documented mood benefits via norepinephrine release. Start with 15 seconds if needed. Build up.
Protein still 140gMPS remains elevated for 24–72 hours post-training. Rest days are still muscle-building days — your body is remodelling the tissue. Hitting protein targets on rest days is as important as on training days.
Mobility workFor your weak shoulders specifically: 10 min band work — face pulls, pull-aparts, and wall slides on rest days. This builds the stabiliser base that allows Phase 2 shoulder reintroduction safely.
Sleep protocol — your most powerful recovery tool
Target: asleep by 2am
Start with this single shift
Do not try to fix 5am sleep overnight — it causes insomnia. Push back 30 minutes every 5 days. From 5am → 4:30am → 4am → 3:30am → 3am → 2am over 5 weeks. This is physiologically sound and achievable.
1 hour before sleep
Wind-down sequence
Take Magnesium glycinate 300mg. Dim all screens or switch to night mode. No food after 10:30pm. If THC is being used, this is the acceptable window — keep it here, not earlier in the evening when it will disrupt daytime function.
Why this matters
Growth hormone and the early sleep window
Growth hormone (GH) is secreted in its largest daily pulse in the first slow-wave sleep cycle. The earlier this cycle starts, the more aligned it is with the natural GH peak. Shifting sleep from 5am to 2am means capturing 3 hours of peak anabolic recovery you are currently missing entirely. This single change will accelerate your physical transformation more than most training variables.
Sleep environment
Optimise the room
Temperature 18–20°C is optimal for sleep — cooler than you think. Blackout curtains or an eye mask if needed. No phone screen in the last 30 minutes. Bedroom = sleep + sex only — working or scrolling in bed trains your brain to be alert there.

Lung Recovery Protocol

Daily lung support habits
Diaphragmatic breathing — 5 min daily
Lie on your back. Place one hand on chest, one on belly. Breathe so only the belly hand rises. Inhale 4 counts, exhale 6 counts. This strengthens the diaphragm, increases lung volume, and reduces residual air volume in the lower lobes. Do this every morning. Watch tutorial →
Eat broccoli 4–5x per week
Sulforaphane in broccoli activates the Nrf2 pathway — the body's master cellular detoxification system. Studies in smokers show it activates lung detox enzymes within hours. Chop broccoli and let it sit 5 minutes before cooking to maximise sulforaphane content.
2 cups green tea daily
EGCG in green tea is one of the best-studied natural compounds for lung protection in smokers. It neutralises free radicals in bronchial tissue and reduces oxidative stress markers. Replace one coffee with green tea.
Humidify your sleep environment
Dry air — common in apartments and particularly relevant for Tbilisi winters — desiccates bronchial mucus and impairs the mucociliary clearance that removes inhaled particles. A simple humidifier running at night reduces this. Targets 40–60% relative humidity.
Take NAC 600mg twice daily
N-Acetyl Cysteine is the most evidence-backed supplement for smokers. It replenishes glutathione, thins bronchial mucus (making it easier to clear), and has been shown to reduce respiratory symptoms in chronic smokers within 4–8 weeks.
Never smoke immediately before exercise
Carbon monoxide from combustion binds haemoglobin with 200x the affinity of oxygen, reducing O2 delivery to working muscles. Wait minimum 45 minutes after any smoking session before training. Pre-workout smoking is the most counterproductive pattern possible.
Cardio frequency — even light cardio helps
The 20-minute daily walk already helps. As training progresses, aerobic conditioning directly strengthens respiratory muscles (diaphragm, intercostals) and increases VO2 max. Each Phase adds more conditioning work for this reason.

Psoriasis Daily Management

Daily skin protocol — morning and evening
Morning showerLukewarm only. Hot water triggers vasodilation and histamine release — both aggravate psoriatic plaques. Use a fragrance-free, sulphate-free body wash. Maximum 10 minutes. Pat dry — do not rub.
Post-shower (3 min)Moisturise immediately. Apply within 3 minutes while skin is still slightly damp. Use a thick fragrance-free emollient — Cetaphil, Eucerin, CeraVe, or equivalent. This is not optional. Moisturising dry psoriatic skin is as important as any medication.
Midday (Tbilisi)Sun therapy. 15–20 minutes of direct midday sun on affected patches, April–September. Build from 10 minutes. Apply SPF 30+ to non-affected skin after 20 minutes. This is UVB phototherapy at no cost.
Post-trainingShower within 20 min. Sweat contains urea and lactic acid that irritate plaques. Same protocol as morning — lukewarm, fragrance-free, moisturise immediately after.
EveningSecond moisturise if dry. Any time skin feels tight or dry — apply again. Skin hydration is the most cost-effective psoriasis management tool available. Dehydrated psoriatic skin flares faster and heals slower.
ClothingLoose, breathable fabrics. Friction on psoriatic skin triggers Koebner phenomenon — new plaques appearing at sites of mechanical trauma. Tight clothing, rough fabrics, and elastic waistbands on affected areas all contribute. In Tbilisi heat: linen and cotton are your best options.
