Protein density — most protein per calorie

Ranked by protein per 100 kcal. When you are struggling to hit 140g without blowing your calorie budget, go to the top of this list.

🥇Egg whites — 26g protein per 100 kcal
Cheapest, most bioavailable protein source available. Add 2 egg whites to your whole eggs every morning. Adds 14g protein for 60 kcal.
🥈Cottage cheese — 14g protein per 100 kcal
Casein protein — slow digesting, keeps you full for 4–5 hours. The best late-night snack. 180g = 20g protein, 150 kcal. Always keep this stocked.
🥉Chicken breast — 22g protein per 100 kcal
180g cooked = 40g protein, 185 kcal. The most efficient lean protein for your budget per macro. Batch cook 3–4 breasts at once.
4Tuna in water — 24g protein per 100 kcal
1 tin = 25g protein, 100 kcal, essentially zero fat. Fastest protein fix when you have no time to cook. Keep 6 tins in the cupboard at all times.
5Greek yogurt (full fat) — 11g protein per 100 kcal
150g = 17g protein, 140 kcal. More filling than low-fat because the fat slows gastric emptying. Full fat beats low fat for satiety every time.
6Salmon — 14g protein per 100 kcal
Higher calorie than chicken but brings omega-3 EPA/DHA that nothing else gives you. 3x per week is enough to cover your omega-3 needs. Do not skip it for the cheaper option every time.
Nutracheck — how to use it properly
01
Save meals as templates
Go to My Meals → Create Meal. Build your standard Meal 1 (eggs + oats + Greek yogurt) once. Save it. Every morning tap it and log the whole thing in one tap. Same for Meal 2. This removes the ADHD friction of re-logging every day from scratch.
02
Watch the protein number first — nothing else
Open the app. Look at PROTg remaining. That is your only job. If it is above 100g remaining at 6pm you need to take action immediately. Calories sort themselves out when protein is hit — you cannot eat 140g protein without also consuming enough calories.
03
Log before you eat, not after
Logging after means you have already eaten something you cannot undo. Log what you are about to eat, check the macros, then eat. Takes 30 seconds and prevents the problem entirely.
04
Barcode scanner for everything packaged
Do not manually search packaged food. Tap the barcode icon, scan it, done. Nutracheck UK database is very accurate. For loose food (eggs, chicken, oats) use the search and select the most basic version — no sauces or preparation methods added.
05
The 1,800 floor — never go below this
If your FOOD number drops below 1,800 kcal at any point during the day, you need to eat more. Going under consistently will suppress your metabolism and cost you muscle. Use the remaining kcal display at the bottom — if it shows more than 400 left at 8pm, add a proper meal, not a snack.
06
Consistent device — pick one and stick to it
Nutracheck does sync across devices if you are logged into the same account — unlike the 90-day tracker which is device-local. Make sure you are signed in on both iPad and laptop with the same Nutracheck account and it will sync automatically.
07
Use the Week View to spot patterns
Tap Week View at the top of the diary. This shows your average protein and calories across 7 days. More useful than any single day. If your weekly average protein is above 130g, you are on track even if individual days are inconsistent.

Supplement stacking — what to combine and what to separate
✓Vitamin C + Zinc — always together Stack
Vitamin C significantly increases zinc absorption and vice versa. Take them at the same time with Meal 1. This pairing also accelerates wound healing, skin repair, and immune function simultaneously.
✓Vitamin D3 + K2 — always together Stack
D3 increases calcium absorption. K2 directs that calcium to bones and away from arteries. Taking D3 without K2 long-term risks arterial calcification. They are sold combined — buy the combo version.
✓Omega-3 + Vitamin D3 — good stack Stack
Both fat-soluble, both taken with a fatty meal. The fat from your meal aids absorption of both simultaneously. Omega-3 also increases D3 receptor sensitivity in muscle tissue.
✓Magnesium + Casein before sleep — optimal recovery stack Stack
Magnesium deepens slow-wave sleep. Casein provides a steady amino acid drip for 6–8 hours. Combined, they maximise the growth hormone pulse that fires in deep sleep. This is the most important recovery combination in the entire stack.
✗Zinc + Calcium — separate by 2 hours Separate
Calcium and zinc compete for the same intestinal absorption transporter. Taking them together reduces zinc absorption by up to 50%. Take zinc with Meal 1 and calcium (if supplementing) with Meal 2.
✗Iron + Calcium — never together Separate
Calcium is the most powerful inhibitor of iron absorption. If taking iron (especially relevant for the female profile), take it on an empty stomach with Vitamin C only. No dairy within 2 hours.
✗B-Complex at night Avoid
B vitamins — particularly B6 and B12 — are energising. Taking them at night causes difficulty sleeping and vivid dreams in many people. Morning only, always with food.
✗Creatine + caffeine — not a concern Fine together
Old research suggested caffeine blocked creatine uptake. This has been disproven. Your morning creatine with coffee is completely fine. Take creatine whenever it fits into your routine — consistency of daily intake matters far more than timing.
Quick wins — small changes, immediate impact
⚡The 30g protein breakfast rule
Getting 30g+ protein at Meal 1 sets your hunger hormones (ghrelin and leptin) for the rest of the day. You will naturally eat less junk in the afternoon. Skipping protein at breakfast is the single fastest way to be 80g behind on protein by 6pm.
⚡Add egg whites to everything
2 egg whites = 7g protein, 30 kcal, no taste, no fat. Add them to scrambled eggs, oats, smoothies. The cheapest and most invisible protein boost available.
⚡Greek yogurt replaces sour cream, mayo, and dips
Use full-fat Greek yogurt as a dip, sauce base, or topping instead of sour cream or mayo. Saves 100–200 kcal and adds 10–15g protein. Works with everything savoury.
⚡Drink water before you think you are hungry
Dehydration mimics hunger. Before reaching for a snack between meals, drink 400ml water and wait 10 minutes. If still hungry, eat. This eliminates a significant portion of unplanned snacking — relevant for both the THC appetite spike and ADHD impulsive eating.
⚡Batch cook once a week
Cook 4 chicken breasts, a large pot of rice, and roast a tray of vegetables on the same day. Refrigerate. For the next 4 days Meal 2 takes 2 minutes to assemble. This is the single most powerful habit for consistent protein intake.
⚡Turmeric + black pepper — always together
Curcumin (active compound in turmeric) has less than 1% bioavailability alone. Piperine in black pepper increases its absorption by 2,000%. Always add a pinch of black pepper when using turmeric. It changes nothing about the flavour.
⚡The 10pm rule — nothing after this
Insulin sensitivity drops 30–50% after 10pm. Carbs eaten after this are preferentially stored as fat, not used as energy or glycogen. This is the mechanism behind the late-night eating body fat pattern. Cottage cheese is the one exception — it is protein-only and slow-digesting.
Psoriasis — food that directly affects flares
🔥Foods that trigger or worsen flares
Red meat more than 2x per week (arachidonic acid — pro-inflammatory). Processed seed oils (sunflower, vegetable, canola — high omega-6 that competes with omega-3). Refined sugar and white flour. Alcohol. Nightshades (tomatoes, peppers, aubergine) in some individuals — track if flares correlate.
🧊Foods that reduce flare frequency
Salmon and mackerel (EPA/DHA reduces IL-17 and TNF-α directly). Olive oil daily (oleocanthal has anti-inflammatory effect comparable to low-dose ibuprofen). Broccoli (sulforaphane activates Nrf2 cellular defence pathway). Turmeric with pepper. Berries (anthocyanins reduce systemic oxidative stress).
☀️Tbilisi sun — use it as medicine
15–20 minutes of direct midday sun on psoriasis patches is clinically equivalent to mild UVB phototherapy. April through September in Tbilisi gives you this for free. Build from 10 minutes. Never burn — sunburn triggers a Koebner response (new plaques at trauma sites).
